==================================================
WORKSTREAM A — VARIATION HARDENING
==================================================

OBJECTIVE
Move the current variation implementation from good v1 behavior to commercially robust behavior.

Required improvements:

1. Job linkage support
- Variation should be optionally linkable to a specific job where relevant
- If a variation arises during or because of a specific job/work package, that relationship must be storable and visible
- Do not make job mandatory; keep nullable for broader commercial variations

2. Partial invoicing support
- A variation may be invoiced in multiple parts
- Do NOT treat a variation as fully “invoiced” just because one variation invoice exists
- Track:
  - approved variation amount
  - total invoiced against that variation
  - remaining variation value to invoice

3. Better variation statuses
Review current status model and improve it if needed.
Preferred operational model:
- draft
- sent
- approved
- declined
- partially_invoiced
- fully_invoiced

If introducing both partially_invoiced and fully_invoiced is the safest option, do it.
If you believe status should be derived instead of stored, explain why and implement safely.

4. Prevent over-invoicing at variation level
- Total variation invoices linked to a variation must never exceed the approved variation amount
- Existing guard should remain, but the UI must also clearly show remaining variation value

5. Variation commercial metadata
Add, expose, or confirm these fields where safely possible:
- title
- reason / description
- amountExclGst
- status
- created date
- approved date if available
- linked project
- linked quote
- linked job (nullable)
- total invoiced against variation
- remaining to invoice

Do not overbuild document management in this phase.
Keep it commercially useful and traceable.
